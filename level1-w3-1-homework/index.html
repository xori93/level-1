Exotic Fruits

Guava is a tropical fruit with a sweet, fragrant flavor and a soft, pink or white interior filled with small edible seeds. Native to Central America, it is widely grown in warm climates around the world. Guava is packed with nutrients, especially vitamin C, fiber, and antioxidants, making it a healthy and refreshing snack. It can be eaten raw or used in juices, jams, desserts, and savory dishes. Its distinct aroma and flavor make it a popular choice in many cuisines.

  • Rich in Vitamin C
  • High in Dietary Fiber
  • Contains Antioxidants

Soursop, also known as graviola or guanabana, is a tropical fruit known for its spiky green skin and soft, white, juicy flesh. Native to Central and South America, it has a sweet-tart flavor, often described as a mix of strawberry and pineapple. Soursop is not only enjoyed fresh but is also used in smoothies, desserts, and traditional remedies. Star fruit, also known as carambola, is a unique tropical fruit named for its star-like shape when sliced. It has a crisp texture and a mildly sweet and tangy flavor. Native to Southeast Asia, star fruit is rich in vitamin C, antioxidants, and fiber, and is often enjoyed fresh, juiced, or added to salads and desserts. Its beautiful shape also makes it a popular garnish. While delicious and nutritious, people with kidney issues should avoid it due to its high oxalate content.

  • Named for its star shape when sliced.
  • Crisp texture with a sweet-tart flavor.
  • Native to Southeast Asia, especially the Philippines and Malaysia.
